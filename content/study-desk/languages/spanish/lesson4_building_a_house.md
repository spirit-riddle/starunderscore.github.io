# Lesson 4: Spanish for the Job Site - Building a House

### **Situation**
This lesson focuses on essential Spanish phrases and sentences for communicating on a job site when building a house. It’s tailored for working with Mexican construction workers in Texas, emphasizing clear, practical communication to enhance teamwork and efficiency.

---

## **Particles and Prepositions** (Connecting Words)
- **a** = To / At  
  Example: *Voy **a** la escuela.* (I go to the school.)  
- **de** = Of / From  
  Example: *El martillo es **de** metal.* (The hammer is made of metal.)  
- **en** = In / On / At  
  Example: *El destornillador está **en** la mesa.* (The screwdriver is on the table.)  
- **por** = By / For / Through  
  Example: *Pasa los clavos **por** favor.* (Pass the nails, please.)  
- **para** = For (Purpose or Destination)  
  Example: *Necesito la madera **para** el techo.* (I need the wood for the roof.)  
- **con** = With  
  Example: *Trabajo **con** mi compañero.* (I work with my teammate.)  
- **sin** = Without  
  Example: *No trabajes **sin** guantes.* (Don’t work without gloves.)  

---

## **Pronouns** (Replacing Nouns)

### **Subject Pronouns** (Who is doing the action)
- **Yo** = I  
  Example: ***Yo** necesito medir esto.* (I need to measure this.)  
- **Tú** = You (informal)  
  Example: ***Tú** sube al techo.* (You go up to the roof.)  
- **Él / Ella** = He / She  
  Example: ***Él** trae los ladrillos.* (He is bringing the bricks.)  
- **Nosotros/as** = We  
  Example: ***Nosotros** trabajamos juntos.* (We work together.)  
- **Ellos / Ellas** = They  
  Example: ***Ellos** mezclan el cemento.* (They are mixing the cement.)  
- **Usted** = You (formal)  
  Example: ***Usted** use el casco.* (You use the helmet.)  

---

### **Object Pronouns** (What is affected by the action)
- **Lo / La** = It (masculine / feminine)  
  Example: *Pásame **lo**.* (Pass it to me.)  
- **Me** = Me  
  Example: *Ayúdame con esto.* (Help me with this.)  
- **Te** = You  
  Example: *¿Te puedo ayudar?* (Can I help you?)  
- **Nos** = Us  
  Example: *¿Nos puedes traer los materiales?* (Can you bring us the materials?)  
- **Les** = Them  
  Example: *Dales las instrucciones.* (Give them the instructions.)  

---

## **Adjectives** (Describing Words)

### **Common Adjectives for the Job Site**
- **Grande** = Big  
  Example: *Trae el destornillador **grande**.* (Bring the big screwdriver.)  
- **Pequeño/a** = Small  
  Example: *Esta pieza es muy **pequeña**.* (This piece is very small.)  
- **Fuerte** = Strong  
  Example: *La viga debe ser **fuerte**.* (The beam must be strong.)  
- **Duro/a** = Hard  
  Example: *El cemento está muy **duro**.* (The cement is very hard.)  
- **Limpio/a** = Clean  
  Example: *Mantén el área **limpia**.* (Keep the area clean.)  
- **Suelto/a** = Loose  
  Example: *Este tornillo está **suelto**.* (This screw is loose.)  
- **Nivelado/a** = Level  
  Example: *La pared está bien **nivelada**.* (The wall is well-leveled.)  
- **Resbaloso/a** = Slippery  
  Example: *Cuidado, el suelo está **resbaloso**.* (Careful, the ground is slippery.)

## **Adverbs** (Modifying Verbs)

### **Common Adverbs**
- **Rápidamente** = Quickly  
  Example: *Haz esto **rápidamente**.* (Do this quickly.)  
- **Cuidadosamente** = Carefully  
  Example: *Trabaja **cuidadosamente**.* (Work carefully.)  
- **Siempre** = Always  
  Example: ***Siempre** usa el casco.* (Always wear the helmet.)  
- **Nunca** = Never  
  Example: ***Nunca** fumes cerca del cemento.* (Never smoke near the cement.)  

---

## **Verb Conjugation Basics**

### **Regular Present Tense Endings**
- **-ar Verbs**  
  Example: Trabajar (to work)  
  - Yo **trabajo** = I work  
  - Tú **trabajas** = You work  
  - Él/Ella **trabaja** = He/She works  
  - Nosotros **trabajamos** = We work  
  - Ellos/Ellas **trabajan** = They work  

- **-er Verbs**  
  Example: Comer (to eat)  
  - Yo **como** = I eat  
  - Tú **comes** = You eat  
  - Él/Ella **come** = He/She eats  
  - Nosotros **comemos** = We eat  
  - Ellos/Ellas **comen** = They eat  

- **-ir Verbs**  
  Example: Vivir (to live)  
  - Yo **vivo** = I live  
  - Tú **vives** = You live  
  - Él/Ella **vive** = He/She lives  
  - Nosotros **vivimos** = We live  
  - Ellos/Ellas **viven** = They live
